About Me

Hey there! I'm Shayan Khalkhali, an electrical engineering grad who simply loves to fix and improve gadgets with a knack for tinkering, and automating things.

The image above is a little story from my high school days that sparked my passion for electronics. I had an old laptop that was always running hot and shutting down. My parents didn't know what to do with it and left it to me to figure out. The problem? A busted fan. I got a spare one from a friend, but it wasn't the right size. That didn't stop me. I adapted it to fit by shaving off the excess frame and then putting it back together, not just once, but for the second time (Since my first attempt to fix it was to disconnect and reconnect the fan's wires.)

That experience with the laptop wasn't just a repair job; it was what felt like the beginning of my journey in engineering. Now, I bring that same enthusiasm and hands-on approach to everything I do, whether it's designing new gadgets or finding ways to make existing ones better.

Despite the complexity, I found the entire process intriguing and fun. Each step of disassembly and reassembly taught me valuable lessons about the intricate workings of hardware. Successfully fixing the fan and bringing the laptop back to life was a rewarding experience that solidified my love for engineering.

Today, I bring the same dedication and enthusiasm to every project I undertake. Whether it's designing antennas, automating processes, or diving deep into the components of a device, I'm always eager to solve problems and innovate.

Thanks for stopping by, and feel free to explore my portfolio to see more of my work and projects!
